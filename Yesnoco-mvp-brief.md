# YESNOCO — MVP BRIEF (Revenue-First)

## Core Idea (internal)

A lightweight web app where people engage in **structured Yes/No duels**, with tipping tied to moments of clarity, skill, and representation.

## Core Promise (user-facing copy)

> **“Ask better questions. Get clearer answers.”**
> **“Duel it out—Yes, No, or Comment.”**

---

# 1) MVP GOAL

Validate two things:

1. People will **engage with Y/N constraint**
2. People will **tip during or after duels**

Success = even small revenue ($50–$200) + repeat participation

---

# 2) CORE FEATURES (ONLY WHAT MATTERS)

## A) Question Card (the heart)

**UI:**

* Question (required Y/N format)
* Author
* Buttons:
  → **Yes**
  → **No**
  → **Comment**

**Microcopy:**

* Placeholder:

  > “Ask a clear Yes/No question they can’t dodge.”

* Subtext:

  > “Good questions force honest answers.”

---

## B) Answer + Challenge Flow

After someone clicks Yes or No:

**Composer appears:**

* “Explain your answer (optional)”
* “Challenge back with a Yes/No question”

**Microcopy:**

> “Answer first. Then ask better.”

---

## C) Duel Thread

Simple linear chain:

* Q → A → Q → A

No branching. No complexity.

**Top label:**

> ⚔️ Duel in progress

**If inactive:**

> 🦗 No response yet

---

## D) Tip Button (CRITICAL)

Always visible on:

* question cards
* answers

**Primary CTA:**

> 💸 Tip

**On click:**

* $1 / $3 / $5 quick options

---

# 3) BUILT-IN TIPPING HOOKS (INLINE COPY)

These appear contextually—not all at once.

## After a strong question:

## After a weak/dodged response:

> “Good duel. Back it.”

## Passive footer under every duel:

## On tip modal:
